CRISTIANO RONALDO

Cristiano Ronaldo (born February 5, 1985, Funchal, Madeira, Portugal) is a Portuguese football (soccer) forward who is one of the greatest players of his generation. Ronaldo is the winner of five Ballon d’Or awards (2008, 2013, 2014, 2016, and 2017), and in 2024 he became the first men’s player to score 949 career goals in official matches.

HISTORY

CAREER AT SPORTING CP

Cristiano Ronaldo played one season for Sporting CP (2002–2003) after rising through the youth ranks, making his professional debut at age 17. During his time there, he scored 5 goals in 31 appearances across all competitions before being transferred to Manchester United for a reported £12.24 million. in August 2003.

CAREER AT MANCHESTER UNITED

Cristiano Ronaldo had two distinct spells at Manchester United, establishing himself as a legend during his first tenure and returning for a second, shorter stint. In his initial period from 2003-2009, he scored 118 goals in 292 appearances, won multiple trophies including the Champions League, and earned the Ballon d'Or. He returned to the club in 2021, scoring 27 goals in 54 appearances across two seasons before his departure in 2022.

CAREER AT REAL MADRID

Cristiano Ronaldo had a highly successful nine-year career at Real Madrid, scoring a club-record 450 goals in 438 appearances, winning 16 trophies including four Champions League titles, and breaking numerous individual records. He joined in 2009 for a then-record fee and left in2018 for Juventus

CAREER AT JUVENTUS

Cristiano Ronaldo played for Juventus from 2018 to 2021, scoring 101 goals in 134 appearances and winning five trophies: two Serie A titles, two Super Cups, and one Coppa Italia. He was a key player for the Italian club, despite the team not winning the Champions League during his tenure.

CAREER AT AL NASSR

So far, Cristiano Ronaldo has scored 79 goals in 82 appearances for Al Nassr FC in the Saudi Pro League. Overall, he has scored 105 goals in 118 games for the Riyadh-based team. Cristiano Ronaldo scored his 100th goal for Al Nassr in the Saudi Super Cup 2025 final against Al Ahli in Hong Kong China.
